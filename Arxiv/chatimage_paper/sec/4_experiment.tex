We evaluate \method{} along two axes that matter for an interactive visual
answer: \textbf{pipeline completion} (does the system produce a usable
generated image and hotspot set for the question?) and \textbf{visual-alignment
accuracy} (do the clickable hotspots land on the rendered content they claim
to describe?). All reported numbers in this section come from a single
real-provider run over the benchmark, followed by an additional manual audit
of visible hotspot alignments. We distinguish three levels of success: a
\emph{generated} run completes the full text-to-image-to-hotspot pipeline; a
\emph{strict-gate} run also passes the automatic grounding and mask-quality
checks used before promotion to the public demo page; and a \emph{manual
alignment} count records whether inspected visible hotspots are judged to land
on the intended visual region.

We assemble a benchmark of 30 questions designed to exercise different visual
modes and content types, summarized in Table~\ref{tab:benchmark_split}. The
current public showcase contains selected outputs from the same \method{}
workflow after strict visual-alignment screening: a hand-drawn West Lake tour
map and daily-life illustrated scenes such as healthy breakfast options,
boutique coffee shop, sunny reading nook, independent record-store corner, and
indoor plant-care corner. Map questions test geographic region placement and
route-like scenic areas, while scene questions test object localization in
less rigidly structured images. The broader benchmark also includes
infographic-style planning and decision-support prompts to measure how compact
analytical regions behave. Only runs that pass the current strict grounding
and mask-quality gate are promoted to the public demo page. Each question is
run through the full \method{} pipeline with real text, image, and vision
providers.

Because the grounding step is provider-agnostic
(Section~\ref{sec:method_alignment}), individual benchmark items may be served
by different grounding backends. We therefore report both aggregate completion
metrics and the per-module alignment source distribution, so the contribution
of each backend (LocateAnything-3B, MiMo-Vision, SAM3-refined planned bounds,
and planned fallback) is visible. Representative qualitative results are shown
in Figure~\ref{fig:qualitative}.

\subsection{Pipeline Results}
\label{sec:experiments_pipeline}

\input{table/main_results}

Out of 30 benchmark questions, 15 completed the full pipeline, yielding a
generated-image completion rate of $50.0\%$. The remaining 15 failed at the
text-to-image step during the run, primarily because of upstream image API
rate-limiting rather than downstream hotspot construction. The automatic
strict visual-alignment gate accepted 4 of the 30 questions ($13.3\%$). This
gate is intentionally conservative: it requires all hotspots in a case to
carry a real grounding source and a valid organic mask preview, so a single
missing or weak region rejects the whole case.

Since case-level strict gating can understate partially useful alignments, we
also perform a manual audit over visible hotspots in the generated outputs.
In this audit, \textbf{17 of 24 inspected hotspots} are judged correctly
aligned with their intended visual region, yielding a conditional manual
alignment success rate of \textbf{$70.8\%$}. We use this number as the
reported visible-hotspot alignment figure and keep it separate from the
stricter case-level gate.

\subsection{Alignment Source Analysis}
\label{sec:experiments_sources}

\input{table/ablation_results}

Table~\ref{tab:ablation_results} reports the grounding source distribution
for the 90 hotspots produced by completed benchmark runs. Most generated
hotspots are supplied by primary visual grounding: MiMo-Vision accounts for
50 hotspots ($55.6\%$), while LocateAnything contributes another 11 hotspots
when combining layout-guided and crop-based calls ($12.2\%$). SAM3-refined
planned regions account for 16 hotspots ($17.8\%$), and planned fallback is
used for 11 hotspots ($12.2\%$). This distribution reflects the robustness
design in Section~\ref{sec:method_alignment}: the system keeps modules that
can be grounded by a vision backend, refines plausible planned regions when a
mask is available, and falls back to planned bounds only when stronger signals
are unavailable.

\subsection{Per-Mode Analysis}
\label{sec:experiments_permode}

\input{table/task_breakdown}

Table~\ref{tab:task_breakdown} breaks down completion and strict-gate results
by visual mode. Scene questions complete most often in this run (6/10) and
also produce most strict-gate passes (3/10), which matches the current
strength of object-centric visual grounding models. Map questions complete at
3/5 and contribute one strict pass, while infographic questions complete at
6/15 but produce no strict-gate passes. Infographics remain challenging
because their regions are often compact, text-heavy, and semantically close to
one another; future work should add stronger OCR-aware grounding and
polygon-level hit regions for these dense layouts.
